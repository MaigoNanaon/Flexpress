Flexpéro: Flexible Expressive Zero-shot Speech Refinement via Interactive In-context Learning

Abstract

Controlling speech expressiveness has emerged as a critical research frontier in speech generation, focusing on synthesizing natural, human-like speech that accurately conveys intended psychological and emotional states. While numerous large models have demonstrated sufficient zero-shot controllability by conditioning the acoustic model on reference speech—which provides cues on speaker identity and style—they often fall short of precisely meeting desired emotional or prosodic targets. This limitation highlights the need to bridge the gap between coarse-grained style/timbre transfer and fine-grained emotional control. In this paper, we propose a novel speech refinement method that can flexibly and interactively enhance expressiveness on undesirable speech segments in zero-shot scenarios. We build a voice conversion model that harnesses the power of in-context learning, enabling users to specify emotion states and a set of "keyframes" and use them as conditionals to achieve precise control over prosody. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach achieves both zero-shot generation and fine-grained control without compromising speech quality or speaker similarity.
